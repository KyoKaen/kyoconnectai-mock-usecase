Innovative Classrooms in Peru
An AI Platform to Support STEAM Education

Executive Summary

Innovative Classrooms in Peru is a context-sensitive, AI-powered educational platform supporting Peruvian schoolteachers in delivering STEAM education across all grade levels. It addresses the lack of curriculum-aligned digital tools, Spanish-language resources, and the time constraints that hinder innovative lesson planning.

By integrating AI with Peru’s national curriculum, the platform enables smart lesson planning, adaptive content suggestions, and intuitive workflows tailored to each classroom’s context. Its hybrid economic model (freemium for individual teachers, subscriptions for schools) ensures both accessibility and sustainability.

Problem

Peruvian teachers struggle with limited access to training, digital resources, and curriculum-aligned materials—making STEAM lesson planning fragmented and time-consuming. 66% of students fall below minimum proficiency in mathematics (PISA 2022), highlighting an urgent need for better classroom tools and support.

Solution

Our platform empowers teachers with AI-assisted lesson planning, curriculum alignment, adaptable project templates, and collaborative tools—entirely in Spanish and tailored for Peru’s national standards. It reduces planning time, boosts teacher confidence, and makes cross-disciplinary projects more accessible to all schools.

Market & Competition

Market: Over 65,000 schools and 545,000 teachers in Peru. Initial focus is on 3,300+ private, full-level urban schools—early adopters of educational technology.
Competition: Existing tools lack Peruvian curriculum alignment, local language support, or dynamic AI-powered lesson generation. Our solution fills this gap, providing unique value for the region.

Roadmap
  • 2025: MVP development, pilot programs in select schools
  • 2026: Early adopters (10–100 schools), product refinement
  • 2027–28: National rollout, regional expansion, new features
Financials

Revenue streams: Institutional subscriptions (~$1,100/year), teacher upgrades (~$60/year), workshops, and implementation services.
Break-even projected in Year 4 (2028), with early funding needs of $100,000 for MVP and go-to-market.
